About us

At Share A Meal, we believe that no meal should go to waste while people go hungry. Our platform connects restaurants, grocery stores, and individuals with surplus food to those in need, ensuring every meal finds a home.

Our Mission

Millions of tons of food are wasted every year, while countless individuals struggle with hunger. Our mission is to bridge this gap by making food donation easy, efficient, and impactful. We provide a real-time tracking system, ensuring donated food is delivered swiftly and safely to local charities, shelters, and people in need.

Our History & Impact in Mumbai (2024)

Mumbai, a city deeply rooted in spirituality, has long upheld the sacred tradition of Annadanam (food donation). At Share A Meal, we have worked closely with Hindu temples, dharamshalas, charitable trusts, and NGOs to ensure that no prasad or surplus food goes to waste while many still go hungry.

In February 2024, during Maha Shivratri, we collaborated with Babulnath Temple and Mumbadevi Temple, collecting over 6,000 meals from temple donations. These meals were then distributed to orphanages and homeless shelters through Annapurna Seva Trust, ensuring that the blessings of prasad reached those in need.

As part of our Ram Navami Feast in April 2024, we received a generous donation of prasad and fresh meals from ISKCON Juhu, amounting to over 12,000 servings. This food was shared with underprivileged families in Dadar, Sion, and Ghatkopar, through our trusted NGO partners Seva Kitchen Mumbai and Roti Ghar NGO.

During Navratri in October 2024, the Shri Siddhivinayak Temple Trust contributed an astounding 25,000 meals to support devotees and the underprivileged. These meals were sent to Mumbai Roti Bank and Snehalaya NGO, where they were distributed to shelters, old-age homes, and families in need across the city.

In January 2024, as part of our Makar Sankranti Food Drive, we partnered with Shree Gaushala Trust, collecting fresh meals, milk, and grains. These offerings were shared with Annamrita Foundation and Satya Sai Seva Sangh, ensuring that temple workers and poor families received nourishment during the festival.

Through these efforts, we continue to uphold the principles of Sanatan Dharma, ensuring that sacred food reaches those who need it most.
